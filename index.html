Sviatoslav's Choir

Izbornyk Svyatoslav - is one of the oldest written monuments of the Old Slavic language on the territory of Ukraine-Russia. It contains articles of various content - religious, household, educational, literary, etc. It was written down, probably in Kyiv, for Prince Svyatoslav from the corresponding Old Bulgarian collection, which was a translation from the Greek original for the Bulgarian Tsar Simeon. The monument is preserved in the State Historical Museum in Moscow. It was published in 1880.

Kyiv-Pechersk paterik

Kyiv-Pechersk Paterik is a collection of stories about the monks of the Kyiv-Pechersk Monastery, founded in the middle of the 11th century. Paterik was compiled in the 13th century on the basis of correspondence between Bishop Simon of Volodymyr-Suzdal and Polycarp, a monk of the Pechersky Monastery. The wonderful literary form and the interest of Paterik's stories determined his constant popularity among readers until the 18th century.

Ostroh Bible

The Ostrog Bible is a landmark of book printing in Ukraine, the first complete edition of the canonical biblical text in the Church Slavonic language. The full title of the book is "The Bible, the Old Testament and the New Testament in the Slovene language". It was printed during 1580â€“1581 in the own estate of the famous Ukrainian magnate and political figure, Prince Vasyl Kostyantyn of Ostrog, in Ostroza (now the city of Ostrog, Rivne region) in a printing house specially organized for the production of Bibles. The first Ukrainian printer Ivan Fedorov (Fedorovych) (born in 1583) was invited to print the Bible. This is the last known edition of the famous printer.

Peresopnytsia Gospel

The Tale of the Temporal Years is a chronicle compiled in Kyiv at the beginning of the 12th century, a monument of historiography and literature of Kyivan Rus. The original "Tales of Bygone Years" has not survived to this day. Only the later lists have survived. "List" means "rewriting" from another source. "The Tale of Bygone Years" is the first monument in Kyivan Rus, in which the history of the state is shown against a broad background of world events. Covers the history of Eastern Slavs and princely power, the establishment of Christianity in Russia, contains stories about the emergence of Slavic writing, reflects the moods of various social strata. Entries are submitted annually. Tales, stories, tales, legends are used.
